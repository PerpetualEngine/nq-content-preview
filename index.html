Subject: The doorway is gratitude

Beloved,

There was a season when pain would arrive and I would reach for anything to soften it. Sad music to cry along to. A distraction to take the edge off. Anything so I would not have to feel the whole of it.

Then one night I turned the music off and let myself feel it all the way down. The grief, the breaking of the story, the death of who I thought I was. I stayed with it. And at the very bottom, I found something I did not expect. Gratitude.

Pain hurts, and that part is honest. Suffering is the resistance to it, the bracing, the looking away. When we stop bracing and let the feeling move through, it does what every emotion is born to do. It passes, and it leaves a gift.

There is nothing too big or too heavy to be grateful for, once you have let yourself feel it fully. That is the portal to rebirth. The doorway is gratitude.

Just for today, when something aches, let yourself feel it all the way down, and notice what is waiting underneath.

Love,
Natalie

A practice

A "just for today" invitation to carry with you.

Drawn from the way you describe meeting your own pain in the video.

Just for today

Just for today, when an ache rises, turn off the distraction.

Let yourself feel it all the way down. The grief, the heaviness, the part of the story that is ending. Stay with it a little longer than is comfortable, the way you would stay with a friend who is crying.

When you reach the bottom of it, ask one quiet question. Can I find one thing here to be grateful for? Let the answer rise from your body, not your mind.

This is how pain becomes a doorway.

The doorway is gratitude

There is a doorway most of us walk past, because it is hidden behind the very thing we spend our lives trying to avoid.

We are never taught how to meet pain. In school we learn calculus and the capitals of the world, and almost nothing about what to do when the heart breaks. So when the ache comes, we do the only thing we know. We numb it. We reach for sex, for a drink, for noise, for anything that will take the edge off. And in the numbing, the pain does not leave. It settles into the body and waits.

Here is the distinction that changed everything for me. Pain and suffering are two different things. Pain hurts, and it is honest. Suffering is the resistance to the pain, the bracing against it, the story that this should not be happening to me. Often the heaviest thing we carry is the years we spend refusing to feel the first wound.

Some time ago a marriage of thirteen years ended. I went to bed in so much pain and put on sad music so I could cry. Then I noticed the music was doing my feeling for me, keeping me one step removed from what was actually there. So I turned it off, and I stayed in silence with it for days. I let myself feel all of it. The grief, the breaking of the story, the death of who I thought I was.

And at the bottom of that feeling, I found gratitude. The real kind, the kind that arrives only once you have let yourself feel everything else first. There is nothing too big, too extreme, too traumatic to be grateful for, once it has been fully felt. That gratitude was a portal. It was the light at the end of the tunnel everyone speaks of, and the doorway was thankfulness.

Think of the Japanese art of kintsugi, where broken pottery is bound back together with gold and becomes more beautiful than it was before it broke. Your scars can be the gold. Every wound I have lived through, and I have lived some big ones, I needed in order to become who I am. Every loss, every gain, everyone who came and left, part of the plan of a greater good.

So the next time the ache arrives, try meeting it rather than muffling it. Feel it all the way down. Stay until you reach the gratitude underneath. That is the doorway. The only way out is in.
